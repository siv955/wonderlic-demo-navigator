# PRODUCT_RULES.md

These rules govern every archetype page, every product moment, and every future content edit. Treat as non-negotiable.

## Product-surface scope

1. **Select is only for external hiring candidates.** Never describe Select as a tool for internal promotion candidates, internal mobility, current employees, or existing managers.
2. **Develop is for current employees and managers.** All coaching stories live here.
3. **Team Dynamics is for current teams.** Never use it to evaluate an external candidate.

## Behavioral Interview Guides

4. Behavioral Interview Guides are **Wonderlic-built**, **prebuilt**, **role-related**, **competency-tied** interview guides used to evaluate candidates consistently.
5. **Do not invent Behavioral Interview Guide questions.** Reference the capability; do not write custom "example questions" as if Wonderlic generates them on the fly.

## New Hire Success Tips

6. New Hire Success Tips are for **faster ramping** and **more efficient onboarding** after an external candidate is hired. They are a handoff from selection into onboarding — not a promotion / internal mobility tool.

## Business pain framing

7. **The behavior gap is not the business pain.** The behavior gap (low Diligence, low Influence, etc.) is the **mechanism**.
8. **The business pain is the operational or commercial consequence**: missed revenue, stalled deals, slowed production, missed targets, quality issues, customer impact, turnover, manager time consumed, HR escalation.
9. Use the Business Impact Ladder pattern:
   - **Observable pattern** (what you see)
   - **Operational consequence** (what breaks in the workflow)
   - **Business pain** (the commercial or organizational cost)
   - **Wonderlic value** (how Wonderlic helps)

## Language guardrails

10. **Do not overclaim prediction.** Wonderlic informs; it does not predict outcomes deterministically.
11. **Do not use deterministic language** ("will fail", "guaranteed to", "always", "never" applied to individuals).
12. **Do not make low scores sound like character flaws.** Low is a role-fit or behavior signal, not a moral judgment or leadership ceiling.
13. **Do not use em dashes (—) in user-facing copy.** Use commas, periods, or "to" / "or" instead.

## Coaching tone

14. Accountability is **clarity, expectation-setting, and follow-through** — not harshness.
15. Every low-attribute story should be framed as a **coachable behavior**, not a person to replace.

## Copy hygiene

16. Scripts should read the way a rep would say them out loud. No corporate-abstract language.
17. Every script and recap bullet block should be copyable (copy-to-clipboard button present).
